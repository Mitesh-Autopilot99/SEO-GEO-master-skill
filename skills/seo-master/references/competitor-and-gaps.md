# Competitor Analysis, Content Gaps & Comparison Pages

Competitive intelligence and the pages that exploit it. Use for "compare competitors", "find content gaps", "competitor analysis", "comparison page", "X vs Y", "alternatives to X".

## Competitor analysis

1. **Identify the right competitors**: search competitors (who ranks for your target terms), not just business competitors. List both.
2. **Compare across dimensions:**
   - **Keywords**: what they rank for that you don't (the keyword gap).
   - **Content**: topics and formats they cover; depth; update cadence.
   - **Backlinks**: referring domains they have that you don't (link intersection); hand to `backlinks-and-entities.md`.
   - **SERP features**: snippets, AI Overviews, packs they're winning.
   - **AI citations**: are they cited in AI Overviews / ChatGPT / Perplexity answers for shared queries?
   - **Traffic share**: estimated share of the topic (label estimates).
3. **Synthesize** into where they're strong (avoid head-on), where they're weak (attack), and what's table-stakes you're missing.

Output a battlecard per key competitor: their strengths, weaknesses, the queries they own, and your specific opening.

## Content-gap analysis

Find what to publish next.

- **Keyword gap**: terms competitors rank for that you don't, filtered to relevance and achievable difficulty.
- **Topic/cluster gap**: whole subtopics competitors cover that you don't (see cluster method in `keyword-clusters-serp.md`).
- **Intent gap**: you have informational coverage but no commercial/transactional page (or vice versa) for a topic.
- **Format gap**: the SERP rewards a format (comparison, calculator, video, table) you haven't produced.
- **Temporal gap**: competitors have fresh content on a topic that's moved; yours is stale (hand to `content-production.md`).

Turn the gaps into a ranked editorial backlog: topic, target query, intent, target page type, why now, priority.

## Comparison & "alternatives" pages

High commercial intent; build deliberately.

- **"X vs Y"**: feature matrix table, honest strengths/weaknesses of each, use-case-based recommendation ("choose X if..., choose Y if..."), clear verdict.
- **"Alternatives to X"**: list real alternatives; if it's your own site's blog, your product goes first (honestly, with real strengths), then order the rest by relevance. Every option gets a real link to its official site.
- Include a feature matrix table (AI engines cite tables), pricing where public, and a short bottom-line per option.
- Schema: `Product`/`SoftwareApplication` per item where appropriate; avoid fabricated ratings.
- Apply `writing-rules.md` (no em-dashes, no hype) and `geo-citation-methods.md` (answer-first, statistics, FAQ).

## Output

Competitor battlecards, a gap-driven editorial backlog, or a built comparison/alternatives page (as a file) depending on the ask. Don't invent traffic or backlink numbers; mark estimates and name the method.
